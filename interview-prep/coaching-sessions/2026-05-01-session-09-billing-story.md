## Billing Story — First Delivery (CARL Structure — Staff Mode)

**Context:**
When I took on the EM role for the billing team, I found a bunch of problems right away. The team had no unified development process. Each member didn't know their responsibility or what they should do, so they worked individually. There was also technical debt — the tech lead was pushing fixes directly to production without addressing the root cause.

**Action:**
So I scoped the problems through one-on-ones with each member and planned a unified development workflow for the team. I introduced design review, sprint tracking, testing before production, and an incident reporting process. I also became the coordinator between the project manager, tech lead, and team members. And I helped the team refactor the system architecture to address the root cause, reduce complexity, and eliminate silos.

**Result:**
The team started working more smoothly. They could discuss issues together instead of fixing things individually. They learned from each other. The engineering culture became more transparent. No more silos. PM and tech lead worked together more smoothly. We delivered on time, and stakeholder confidence improved. Technical debt was reduced and system capability increased.

**Learning:**
What I learned is that one-on-one conversations are a very useful management technique. They help you understand what engineers actually think. Every engineer has a different perspective on the same problem. As an EM, your job is to remove blockers, make the team transparent, and help them work smoothly together.

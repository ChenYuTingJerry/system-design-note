## Compressed 4-Layer Story Structure (6 sections)

Clarified the structure of the compressed 3-minute story into six named sections:

**Section 1: Opening (30 seconds)**
Who you are, what problem you identified, why you stepped in.

**Section 2: Constraints (60 seconds)**
Five hard constraints that shaped the decision. Name each one + why it mattered.

**Section 3: Alternatives (45 seconds)**
Three approaches you could have chosen. For each one, name it + what it sacrifices.

**Section 4: Your choice + Tradeoffs (30 seconds)**
Why strangler fig won. What it optimizes for vs. sacrifices.

**Section 5: Counterfactual (15 seconds)**
What would have to change for you to pick differently.

**Section 6: Result (30 seconds)**
What you actually delivered over time. Specific outcomes + metrics.

## Looping Pattern Analysis

**When looping occurs:**
- Under fatigue (after 30+ minutes of continuous practice)
- When trying to remember exact wording instead of speaking from ideas
- On transition phrases ("the other one is", "and and", "they had to they had to")

**When looping does not occur:**
- When fresh (morning sessions)
- When explaining ideas conversationally (not reciting)
- When speaking slowly and deliberately

**Root cause confirmed:** Looping is a fatigue + memorization pattern, not a knowledge gap. The ideas are internalized. The exact wording is not, and should not be.
